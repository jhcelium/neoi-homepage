What is NMN, and what is it supposed to do?

Short answerNMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) is a compound your body uses to make NAD+, a molecule involved in energy metabolism and cellular repair that naturally declines with age. Supplement makers position NMN as a way to support NAD+ levels. Human research is still early, so think of it as a wellness supplement, not a proven treatment.

NMN is one of the most talked-about "longevity" supplements. Here's the grounded version of what it is.

The basics

  • NMN is a precursor to NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide), a coenzyme involved in energy production and DNA/cellular repair.
  • NAD+ levels decline with age, which is why supplementing precursors like NMN has drawn interest.
  • Most evidence so far is from cell and animal studies; human trials are growing but still preliminary.

Community members are candid that NMN is an area of active research rather than settled science — one widely-read r/NMN post is literally a doctor-reviewed list of the "top 7 myths." Treat NMN as a supplement you might explore for general wellness, keep expectations realistic, and read the safety note below.
