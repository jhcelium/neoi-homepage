How do I choose a supplement brand I can trust?

Short answerFavour brands that publish third-party testing or carry independent certifications (USP, NSF, Informed Sport), list exact ingredient amounts, and avoid proprietary-blend vagueness. Be skeptical of huge review counts (some are fake) and of prices that seem too low. Transparency and verification beat marketing every time.

"What brand do you really trust?" is a perennial r/Supplements question — and the answers converge on transparency.

Green flags

  • Third-party testing / certifications: USP, NSF, Informed Sport, or published Certificates of Analysis.
  • Full-dose transparency: exact mg per ingredient, no vague "proprietary blends."
  • Clean labelling and a real, contactable company.

Red flags

  • Too-good-to-be-true pricing and unknown marketplace sellers.
  • Suspiciously perfect reviews — one redditor "stopped trusting brands with big reviews... most are fake or private label."
  • Even respected names get debated, so don't assume premium price equals quality.

The throughline: verification over reputation. A brand that shows its test data earns more trust than one that just looks premium.
