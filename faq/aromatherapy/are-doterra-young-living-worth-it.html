Are doTERRA and Young Living oils worth the price?

Short answerMost independent users say no — these are multi-level-marketing brands whose oils are typically far more expensive than equally pure alternatives. Brands like Plant Therapy, Edens Garden, Revive and Rocky Mountain Oils are widely recommended as high-quality, third-party-tested options at a fraction of the cost.

This comes up constantly, and the community consensus is unusually strong: the actual oil in MLM (multi-level-marketing) brands is often fine, but you are paying a large markup for the sales structure, not for superior quality.

Why people steer away from MLMs

  • Price: comparable purity is available for much less elsewhere.
  • Sourcing claims: "exclusive" sourcing narratives are frequently disputed.
  • Unsafe advice: MLM sales materials sometimes promote internal use, which mainstream aromatherapists warn against.

Brands the community recommends instead

Repeatedly named favourites include Plant Therapy, Edens Garden, Revive, Rocky Mountain Oils and Aura Cacia — companies that publish third-party GC/MS test results and aren't MLMs. As one long-time buyer put it, these are "just as good as the big-name pyramid-scheme brands at a fraction of the price."

The takeaway: judge an oil by its purity testing and your own nose, not by the brand's marketing or price tag.
