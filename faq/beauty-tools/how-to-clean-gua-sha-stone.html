How do I clean and care for a gua sha tool?

Short answerWash it after every use, since it picks up facial oil, serum and skin cells. Use warm water with a little gentle soap, rinse, and dry with a soft cloth. Store it somewhere clean and dry. Stone tools can chip if dropped, so handle them carefully; steel tools are more durable.

A gua sha tool touches your face and glides through oils, so hygiene matters as much as it does for brushes.

Care routine

  • After each use: wash with warm water and a little gentle soap to remove oil and product, then dry with a soft cloth.
  • Disinfect occasionally with a little rubbing alcohol, especially if you've been breaking out.
  • Store dry in a pouch or on a clean surface.

Material notes

  • Stone (jade, quartz, terahertz) can crack or chip if dropped on a hard surface — handle gently.
  • Stainless steel is durable and easy to sanitise.

Keeping it clean means you're massaging with a fresh surface every time, not yesterday's residue.
