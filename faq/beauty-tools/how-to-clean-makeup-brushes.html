How do I clean my makeup brushes?

Short answerWet the bristles with lukewarm water, swirl them on a gentle brush soap or cleanser, rinse until the water runs clear, reshape the head, and lay flat to dry. Keep water away from the metal ferrule so the glue doesn't loosen. A solid cleansing bar makes this fast and mess-free.

Clean brushes apply makeup better and protect your skin from built-up oil and bacteria. The method most people settle on is simple and cheap.

Step by step

  • Wet the bristles under lukewarm (not hot) water, tips pointing down.
  • Lather on a solid brush-cleansing bar or a drop of gentle soap/baby shampoo, swirling in your palm or on a textured mat.
  • Rinse until the water runs clear.
  • Squeeze out excess water, reshape the head, and lay flat to dry.

The single most important rule: keep water out of the ferrule (the metal part). Water that seeps in dissolves the glue over time and causes shedding. Solid cleanser bars are popular because they cut through foundation quickly — reviewers call them the easiest method they've tried, often reviving brushes they'd nearly thrown out.
